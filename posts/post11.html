The free energy principle

On Friston's free energy principle "Things" exist by persisting in time and have a boundary characterized by a 'sparse' coupling with the environment. If things persist, what must they do? (Asks Friston). It depends on the coupling of the system with its environment. A rock in space doesn't need to do much to keep existing. But a living being needs to have the ability to track some properties of their environment and adapt accordingly. Something like a thermostat keeps homeostasis by a simple rule-based system, without modeling itself as part of the world around it. More advanced "things" contain a model of the environment. More advanced "things" include themselves in their internal model of the world. A predictive system capable of acting (such as a brain in a body), in order to keep existing, seeks to minimize prediction error through a combination of improving its internal model of the environment through learning, and by changing the world so that it conforms better with its internal model. This process is free energy minimization because free energy is an upper bound to the largest prediction error, so minimizing free energy is a sure way to optimize prediction performance. Though the idea is that free energy minimization is what systems that persist in time must do in order to keep existing.
