The free energy principle

The free energy principle by Karl Friston is a of physical systems and their dynamics. Free energy is an upper bound to the largest prediction error of a model. So minimizing free energy is a sure way to optimize prediction performance. A predictive system capable of acting (such as a brain), in order to keep existing, seeks minimize free energy via a combination of improving its internal model of the world (by learning on new data) and by changing the world so that it conforms better with its internal model. In the free energy principle, "things" exist by persisting in time and are identified by a sparsity of degrees of freedom coupled with the rest of the universe. This can be generalized to Markov blankets (sets of random variables that are sufficient to infer a given random variable) ##### One can now talk of states of the thing in their own right and the coupling manifests itself in some of the properties of the thing "tracking" properties of the rest (in the sense of being in the markov blanket).
