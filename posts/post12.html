The free energy principle

On Friston's free energy principle "Things" exist by persisting in time. If they persist, what must they do? One thing is that they need to have the ability to track properties of their environment and adapt accordingly. A basic thing like a rock in space doesn't do need to do much to keep existing. Something a little more advanced like a thermostat keeps homeostasis by a simple rule-based system. More advanced "things" contain a model of the environment and they can use it to make predictions. Even more advanced "things" include themselves in their internal model. This process is free energy minimization because free energy is an upper bound to the largest prediction error, so minimizing free energy is a sure way to optimize prediction performance. Though the idea is that free energy minimization is what systems that persist in time must do in order to keep existing.
