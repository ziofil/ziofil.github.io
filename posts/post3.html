Objective and subjective realities

From a physical point of view, the external (let's say objective) world couples with the brain through electromagnetic (EM) effects (the other contributions are gravity and nuclear forces, neither of which should matter at this scale). Some of the effects of the interactions are permanent and result in a modification of the brain (memory, learning, etc..). Others are transitory and their effects eventually dissipate as heat, leaving practically no trace. While all this happens, a mind experiences the qualia that the brain produces as a consequence of the EM interactions.

So the phenomena that a mind experiences are not actually "out there" in the real world, but rather consequences that are ultimately experienced subjectively. There are no "blue doors": there are arrangements of atoms that interact with light in certains ways, and electromagnetic radiation reaching us and knocking onto atoms in our retinas, but nothing in the real world is "blue". The experience of blueness takes place entirely subjectively.

This suggests that at *some point* in the causal chain, qualia are instantiated. When? Where? How?
