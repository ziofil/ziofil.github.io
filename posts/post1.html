Defining the problem

The hard problem of consciousness is to explain how subjective experiences can exist and why they feel the way they do. It is a problem so radically different than any other because it can only be characterized subjectively.

One can come up with secondary problems such as explaining if it is necessary for consciousness to exist (couldn't a brain just do what it does without a mind subjectively experiencing it), or how simple qualia are woven together into the seamless reality that we experience. However, even if we explained these, the hard problem would still remain.
