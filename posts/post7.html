How would a solution look like?

Daniel Dennett likes to imagine a theory of consciousness that solves the hard problem (even if he doesn't like to refer to it as the hard problem) and that never involves the subjective point of view. Let's imagine we had such a theory. What would it look like?

Note that it would be possible to use this theory to say things like: "hey, you see red in the same way I taste onion, no wonder it's not your favourite color". It would also become clear how to endow a system with feelings. "I can make this brick love Mozart". Very powerful ðŸ˜‚.

This theory would allow us to expose the private point of view of a mind and compare your qualia with somebody else's. Though, if this were possible for example by examining the neural correlates of two brains, how would it manage to explain them?

A theory as we intend it can be described in natual language. Can there be a mapping between qualia and natural language? If there was such a mapping, why isn't it possible for Mary to know the color red without ever seeing it?
